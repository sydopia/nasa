[ APOLLO ]

Apollo was the NASA program that resulted in American astronauts' making a total of 11 spaceflights and walking on the moon. The first four flights tested the equipment used in the Apollo Program. Six of the other seven flights landed on the moon.

[ 01.27.1967 ]

Apollo 01

The first manned mission of the United States Apollo program. A cabin fire during a launch rehearsal test on January 27 at Cape Kennedy Air Force Station killed all three crew members and destroyed the Command Module.

[ 10.11.1968 ]

Apollo 07

A human spaceflight mission carried out by the United States. It was the first mission in the United States' Apollo program to carry a crew into space. It was also the first U.S. spaceflight to carry astronauts since the flight of Gemini XII in November 1966.

Learn More

[ 12.27.1968 ]

Apollo 08

The second manned spaceflight mission in the United States Apollo space program and became the first manned spacecraft to leave Earth's orbit, reach the moon, orbit it and return safely to Earth.

Learn More

[ 03.03.1969 ]

Apollo 09

The third manned mission in the United States Apollo space program and the first flight of the Command/Service Module with the Lunar Module. Its three-person crew spent ten days in low Earth orbit testing aspects critical to landing on the Moon.

Learn More

[ 05.18.1969 ]

Apollo 10

The fourth manned mission in the United States Apollo space program, and the second to orbit the moon. It was the F mission: a "dress rehearsal" for the first Moon landing, testing all of the components and procedures short of actually landing.

Learn More

[ 07.20.1969 ]

Apollo 11

The spaceflight that landed the first two humans on the moon. Mission commander Neil Armstrong and pilot Buzz Aldrin, both American, landed the lunar module Eagle. Armstrong became the first to step onto the lunar surface; Aldrin joined him about 20 minutes later.

Learn More

[ 11.14.1969 ]

Apollo 12

The sixth manned flight in the United States Apollo program and the second to land on the Moon. It was launched four months after Apollo 11. Unlike the first landing on Apollo 11, Apollo 12 achieved a precise landing at their expected location.

Learn More

[ 04.11.1970 ]

Apollo 13

The seventh manned mission in the Apollo space program and the third intended to land on the moon. The lunar landing was aborted after an oxygen tank exploded, crippling the Service Module upon which the Command Module had depended. Despite great hardship caused by limited power, the crew returned safely to Earth.

Learn More

[ 01.31.1971 ]

Apollo 14

The eighth manned mission in the United States Apollo program, and the third to land on the Moon. It was the last of the "H missions," targeted landings with two-day stays on the Moon with two lunar EVAs, or moonwalks.

Learn More

[ 07.26.1971 ]

Apollo 15

the ninth manned mission in the United States' Apollo program, the fourth to land on the Moon, and the eighth successful manned mission. It was the first of long stays on the moon, with a greater focus on science. It was also the first mission on which the Lunar Roving Vehicle was used.

Learn More

[ 04.16.1972 ]

Apollo 16

The tenth manned mission in the United States Apollo space program, and the fifth to land on the Moon. The landing spot in the highlands was chosen to allow the astronauts to gather geologically older lunar material than the samples obtained in the first four landings.

Learn More

[ 12.07.1972 ]

Apollo 17

The final mission of NASA's Apollo program and was the last time humans travelled beyond low Earth orbit. The mission broke several records: the longest moon landing, longest total extravehicular activities,largest lunar sample, and longest time in lunar orbit.
